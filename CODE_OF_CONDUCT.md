# Contributor Covenant Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

We as members, contributors, and leaders pledge to make participation in the
SuperJS community a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of age,
body size, visible or invisible disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics,
gender identity and expression, level of experience, education, socio-economic
status, nationality, personal appearance, race, caste, color, religion, or
sexual identity and orientation.

We pledge to act and interact in ways that contribute to an open, welcoming,
diverse, inclusive, and healthy community.

---

## Our Standards

### Positive Behavior

Examples of behavior that contributes to a positive environment for our
community include:

- **Using welcoming and inclusive language** — Choose words that make all
  contributors feel that they belong, regardless of background or experience
  level.
- **Respecting differing viewpoints and experiences** — Disagreement is
  natural; engage with ideas on their merits rather than dismissing them.
- **Accepting constructive criticism gracefully** — Feedback is a gift.
  Receive it with openness and use it to improve the project and yourself.
- **Focusing on what is best for the community** — Prioritize the long-term
  health and growth of SuperJS over individual preferences or short-term gains.
- **Showing empathy toward other community members** — Recognize that
  contributors bring different contexts, time zones, and pressures to the
  table.
- **Acknowledging contributions** — Give credit generously. Celebrate the
  work of others publicly and sincerely.
- **Being patient with newcomers** — Everyone starts somewhere. Guide new
  contributors with care rather than frustration.

### Unacceptable Behavior

Examples of behavior that will not be tolerated include:

- **Sexualized language or imagery** — This includes sexualized comments,
  jokes, imagery, or unwelcome sexual attention or advances of any kind,
  whether in issues, pull requests, chat, or any other project space.
- **Trolling, insulting, or derogatory comments** — Personal attacks,
  belittling remarks, and inflammatory or deliberately provocative language
  serve no constructive purpose.
- **Political or personal attacks** — Keep discussions focused on technical
  and community matters. Attacks on individuals based on political beliefs or
  personal characteristics are prohibited.
- **Public or private harassment** — Continued unwanted contact, intimidation,
  or targeting of an individual through any channel — public or private — is
  unacceptable.
- **Publishing others' private information without permission** — Sharing
  someone's physical address, email, phone number, or any other personally
  identifiable information without their explicit consent ("doxing") is
  strictly forbidden.
- **Sustained disruption of community spaces** — Deliberately derailing
  discussions, spamming, or flooding channels with irrelevant content harms
  everyone.
- **Other conduct reasonably considered inappropriate** — The above list is
  illustrative, not exhaustive. Behavior that a reasonable person would
  consider harmful, exclusionary, or unprofessional is subject to enforcement
  action.

---

## Enforcement Responsibilities

Community maintainers are responsible for clarifying and enforcing the
standards of acceptable behavior outlined in this Code of Conduct. They will
take appropriate and fair corrective action in response to any behavior they
deem inappropriate, threatening, offensive, or harmful.

Maintainers have the right and responsibility to remove, edit, or reject
comments, commits, code, wiki edits, issues, and other contributions that do
not align with this Code of Conduct, and will communicate reasons for moderation
decisions when appropriate.

Maintainers who do not follow or enforce the Code of Conduct in good faith may
face temporary or permanent repercussions as determined by other members of the
project's leadership.

---

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies within all project spaces — including the GitHub
repository, issue tracker, pull requests, discussions, official chat channels,
mailing lists, and any other forum managed by the SuperJS project.

It also applies when an individual is representing the project in public spaces.
Examples of representing the project include using an official project email
address, posting via an official social media account, or acting as an
appointed representative at an online or offline event.

---

## Reporting Violations

Instances of abusive, harassing, or otherwise unacceptable behavior may be
reported to the project maintainers responsible for enforcement at:

**conduct@superjs.dev**

All complaints will be:

- **Received** — acknowledged within 48 hours of submission.
- **Reviewed promptly and fairly** — investigated with due care, treating all
  parties with respect.
- **Kept confidential** — the privacy and security of the reporter will be
  respected throughout the process. The identity of the reporter will not be
  disclosed to the subject of the complaint without explicit consent.

Maintainers are obligated to recuse themselves from handling any complaint in
which they have a conflict of interest.

---

## Enforcement Guidelines

Community maintainers will follow these guidelines when determining consequences
for actions that violate this Code of Conduct:

### 1. Correction

**Community Impact:** Use of inappropriate language or other behavior deemed
unprofessional or unwelcome in the community.

**Consequence:** A private, written warning from the maintainers, providing
clarity around the nature of the violation and an explanation of why the
behavior was inappropriate. A public apology may be requested.

---

### 2. Warning

**Community Impact:** A single incident or series of actions that violates
community standards.

**Consequence:** A public warning documenting the violation. No interaction
with the people involved — including unsolicited interaction with those
enforcing the Code of Conduct — is permitted for a specified period. This
includes avoiding interactions in community spaces as well as external channels
such as social media. Violating these terms may lead to a temporary or
permanent ban.

---

### 3. Temporary Ban

**Community Impact:** A serious violation of community standards, including
sustained inappropriate behavior across multiple interactions or spaces.

**Consequence:** A temporary ban from any sort of interaction or public
communication with the community for a specified period. No public or private
interaction with the people involved — including unsolicited interaction with
those enforcing the Code of Conduct — is permitted during this period.
Violating these terms may result in a permanent ban.

---

### 4. Permanent Ban

**Community Impact:** Demonstrating a pattern of violations of community
standards; sustained harassment of an individual; aggression toward or
disparagement of classes of people; or egregious single acts that are
fundamentally incompatible with community participation.

**Consequence:** A permanent ban from any sort of public interaction within the
project community. This decision is final and may only be reconsidered at the
sole discretion of the core maintainers following a formal appeal process.

---

## Appeals

A person subject to enforcement action may appeal by contacting
**conduct@superjs.dev** with the subject line "Code of Conduct Appeal." Appeals
will be reviewed by a maintainer not involved in the original decision. The
outcome of an appeal is final.
